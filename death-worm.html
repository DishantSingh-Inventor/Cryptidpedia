Mongolian Death worm (olgoi-khorkhoi)

The Mongolian Death Worm is locally known as the olgoi-khorkhoi ("large intestine worm" due to its red colour and bloated shape). It is a subterranean creature reported to inhabit the dry southern regions of the gobi Desert. Nomadic tribes describe it as a suasage like worm capable of burrowing swiftly through loose san dunes.

According to legends the worm possesses highly lethal defensive weapons. It is said to spit yellow, corrosive acid that melts metal on contact and can kill humans from a distance using massive electrical discharges. While early explorers launched expeditions to find it, no physical specimens have ever been captured.

Evidence

  • Deeply ingrained oral traditions among Gobi nomads
  • Consistent descriptions across different regional tribes
  • Unexplained livestock deaths in remote desert sectors

Flaws

  • Annelids cannot survive without water in desert sand
  • No known biological worm can generate high-voltage electricity
  • Highly likely to be misidentified sand boas or amphisbaenians
