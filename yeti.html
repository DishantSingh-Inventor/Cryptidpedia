Yeti (Abominable Snowman)

The Yeti also known as the Abominable Snowman is an ape-like creature said to inhabit the Himalayan region of Nepal, Bhutan, and Tibet. In Tibetan folklore the Yeti is part of the spiritual landscape described as a wild man of the snow who can be either peaceful or highly dangerous depending on how he is approached.

During the 20th century Western explorers became obsessed with finding the Yeti and documenting large, human-like footprints in high-altitude snowfields. Several local monasteries even preserved relics like scalps and hands, which they claimed belonged to Yetis. However, modern scientific evaluations have shed a very different light on these legends.

Evidence

  • Famous photos of footprints in high snow by Eric Shipton
  • Monastery scalp and skin relics preserved for generations
  • Centuries-old folklore across multiple isolated mountain towns

Flaws

  • DNA testing of relics showed they belong to Tibetan and brown bears
  • Footprints are known to warp and enlarge rapidly in melting snow
  • Complete lack of fossil records for large primates in the region
