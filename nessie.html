Loch Ness Monster (Nessie)

The Loch Ness Monster affectionately referred to as Nessie is a legendary aquatic cryptid reputed to inhabit Loch Ness, a deep freshwater loch in the Scottish Highlands. Peoples interest in it peaked in 1934 with the publication of the "Surgeon's Photograph", depicting a long-necked creature rising out of the water.

Believers often argue that Nessie could represent a surviving line of plesiosaurs, prehistoric marine reptiles that supposedly went extinct millions of years ago. However, extensive sonar sweeps, satellite imaging, and environmental DNA analysis of the loch have found no evidence of any large, mysterious creatures.

Evidence

  • Large volume of sonar echoes over decades
  • Significant local tourism industry built on sightings
  • Unexplained wake patterns captured on video

Flaws

  • "Surgeon's Photograph" was confessed to be a toy submarine hoax
  • Loch Ness food supply is too low to support a large predator
  • eDNA sampling found only massive quantities of eel DNA
