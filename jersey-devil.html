Jersey Devil

The Jersey Devil is a legendary creature said to inhabit the Pine Barrens of Southern New Jersey. According to popular folk tales the creature was the 13th child of a local woman named Mother Leeds in 1735. Despised and cursed before birth. the child transformed into a flying beast upon delivery, screeching loudly before escaping up the chimney.

Descriptions of the Jersey Devil are highly bizarre combining a horse-like head, bat-like wings, cloven hooves, clawed hands, and a thin pointed tail. In January 1909, the legend reached a boiling point when thousands of residents reported seeing the creature or finding mysterious, hoof-like tracks in the snow across the state.

Evidence

  • State-wide mass hysteria and sightings in January 1909
  • Unidentified high-pitched screeches heard in Pine Barrens
  • Cloven-hoof tracks found in snow on house rooftops

Flaws

  • Heavily rooted in political rivalry and colonial gossip
  • Descriptions are anatomically impossible for a biological animal
  • Local museums admitts of dressing up kangaroos in fake wings
